## Why Gearbox Work Is Different from Regular Servicing

An oil change or brake pad replacement is routine. A gearbox overhaul is not. It requires:

- **Specialist tooling** — torque converters, CVT belt pullers, hydraulic test benches
- **Electronic diagnostic equipment** — OBDII scanners with transmission-specific read capability
- **Parts sourcing experience** — genuine vs OEM vs pattern parts, brand-specific fluid requirements
- **Workshop experience with your exact car model** — Myvi, Axia, Vios, X-Trail, Civic all have different CVT architectures

A general workshop that "also does gearboxes" is not the same as a workshop built around transmission work.

## What Kulim Drivers Should Watch For

If your car shows any of these symptoms, the gearbox needs attention:

1. **Shuddering or jerking at low speeds** — especially pulling away from traffic lights
2. **Delayed engagement** — pressing D but the car hesitates before moving
3. **High RPM with slow acceleration** — engine revs climbing while speed doesn't follow
4. **Whining or humming sounds** — especially under load or at highway speed
5. **Slipping between gears** — sudden loss of drive, engine free-revving
6. **Transmission warning light** — not to be ignored even if the car drives fine

Kedah's road conditions — hills, long stretches of highway, and frequent stop-start in Kulim town — can accelerate gearbox wear compared to flat urban environments.

## What to Ask a Gearbox Workshop Before You Commit

Before handing over your car:

- **Do you specialise in transmissions, or is this a general workshop?**
- **What diagnostic equipment do you use?**
- **Do you use original or OEM parts? Can I see the parts before installation?**
- **Is there a warranty on the repair?**
- **Can you show me what's wrong before you start?**

A legitimate specialist will welcome these questions. A workshop that rushes you past them is a red flag.
