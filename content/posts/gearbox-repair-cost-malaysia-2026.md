One of the most common questions we receive is: *how much does gearbox repair cost in Malaysia?*

The honest answer is: it depends on the problem, the vehicle, and the quality of the repair. But there are clear benchmarks you should know before calling any workshop.

## Why Gearbox Repair Costs Vary So Much

Before the number breakdown, it is worth understanding why the same problem can attract quotes ranging from RM 800 to RM 6,000 at different workshops.

**Variable factors include:**

- **The diagnosis** — Was the root cause properly identified, or are parts being replaced on assumption?
- **Parts quality** — Original, OEM-specification, or aftermarket? The difference in longevity is significant.
- **Workshop expertise** — Transmission work requires specialist knowledge. A general workshop fixing a CVT for the first time is not the same as a specialist with 15 years of transmission experience.
- **Warranty offered** — A quality rebuild should come with a warranty. If a workshop does not offer one, ask why.

## What Is NOT Included in a Cheap Quote?

When you receive an unusually low gearbox repair quote, ask these questions:

**1. What parts are being used?**
Aftermarket gearbox components are cheaper but generally do not last as long as original or OEM-spec parts. A repair that costs RM 1,500 with aftermarket parts may need redoing in 18 months, while a RM 3,000 repair with original parts lasts 5–8 years.

**2. Is a warranty included?**
A quality repair comes with a warranty. This is not optional — it is a mark of confidence in the work. If no warranty is offered, the workshop is telling you something.

**3. Has the root cause been diagnosed?**
Some workshops quote based on the most common cause of a symptom rather than diagnosing your specific vehicle. This approach often results in parts being replaced that did not need replacing, while the actual problem remains.

## Repair vs. Replace: Which Is Better?

For most vehicles, a proper overhaul of the existing gearbox is the better choice.

| Factor | Overhaul | Replacement Unit |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| Cost | Moderate | Higher (new) or uncertain (reconditioned) |
| Parts | Original/OEM for your unit | Unknown history (reconditioned) |
| Calibration | Factory-spec to your vehicle | May require reprogramming |
| Warranty | Comprehensive | Varies; limited for reconditioned |

A reconditioned (second-hand) replacement gearbox is a cost-saving measure that carries the unknown history of another vehicle's transmission. An overhauled original unit — where each component has been inspected and replaced as needed — is a known quantity.

## How to Evaluate a Gearbox Workshop in Malaysia

Before committing to any workshop for gearbox repair:

1. **Ask for a diagnostic before a quote.** A professional workshop diagnoses before quoting.
2. **Ask specifically what parts will be replaced and what brand they are.**
3. **Ask about the warranty** — duration and what it covers.
4. **Check Google reviews** — look for specific mentions of transmission or gearbox work, not just general reviews.
5. **Ask how long they have been doing transmission work specifically** — gearbox repair is a specialism, not a general mechanical skill.
