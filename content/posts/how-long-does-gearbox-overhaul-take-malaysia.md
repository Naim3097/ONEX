For most Malaysians, the car is not a luxury — it is a necessity. Not being able to drive can mean missed work, expensive Grab rides, and significant daily inconvenience.

So when a gearbox overhaul is needed, how long does it actually take?

## The Short Answer

A professional gearbox overhaul at a dedicated transmission workshop typically takes **3 to 5 working days**.

This covers the most common scenario: a clearly diagnosed automatic or CVT transmission with the required parts available.

## Why It Takes 3–5 Days

The timeline is not arbitrary. Here is what happens during those days:

**Day 1 — Removal and Disassembly**
The transmission is removed from the vehicle — a labour-intensive process requiring proper lifting equipment and specialist knowledge. The unit is then fully disassembled. Components are cleaned, measured, and catalogued.

**Day 2 — Inspection and Parts Sourcing**
After disassembly, the technician inspects every component against manufacturer specifications. The exact parts required are confirmed. If parts need to be ordered (some OEM components are not stocked at every workshop), this is when the wait begins.

**Day 3–4 — Rebuild**
Components are cleaned, worn parts replaced, and the transmission is carefully reassembled to factory tolerances. Seals, gaskets, clutch packs, solenoids, and filters are replaced as required.

**Day 4–5 — Installation, Calibration, and Road Test**
The rebuilt transmission is reinstalled, the ECU adaptation is reset, and the vehicle goes through a comprehensive road test under varied conditions to verify performance before handover.

## What Can Make It Longer

### Parts Availability

This is the most common reason for extended timelines in Malaysia. If your vehicle requires specific OEM components not commonly stocked — certain CVT belt specifications, solenoid packs, or torque converter assemblies — parts may need to be sourced from overseas.

Vehicles less common in Malaysia, such as certain European brands or less popular Japanese models, typically face longer parts lead times.

### Complexity of the Damage

A straightforward clutch pack replacement takes less time than a full rebuild involving damaged planetary gears or a valve body requiring additional machining.

### Workshop Workload

Reputable workshops with consistent quality tend to stay busy. During peak periods, your vehicle may not go in on the same day it is dropped off.

## What to Ask the Workshop Before You Agree

1. **Are the required parts in stock?** — If not, what is the expected lead time?
2. **What is the current workshop schedule?** — How many vehicles are ahead of yours?
3. **What is the realistic completion date?** — Ask for a specific date, not a vague "3 to 5 days"
4. **What does the road test involve?** — A proper road test covers cold start, varied speeds, hills, and acceleration — not just a short loop

## What If You Cannot Be Without Your Car?

Some options to consider:

- **Grab or e-hailing** — For the duration of the repair, the cost is often significantly less than rushing the job or choosing a less competent workshop
- **Workshop loan car** — Some specialist workshops offer a loan vehicle for customers undergoing major repairs; ask about this when booking
- **Plan around the timeline** — If you can arrange transport for one working week, you can choose the right workshop without compromising on quality due to time pressure

## The Most Important Point

Do not choose a workshop based on who promises the fastest turnaround. A gearbox overhaul done in 24 hours with the car "ready tomorrow" is almost always a red flag — it is not physically possible to do the job properly in that time.

Choose based on expertise, transparency, and warranty. Accept the realistic 3–5 working day timeline. Your gearbox will thank you.
