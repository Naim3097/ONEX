Change your engine oil? Everyone knows about that. But change your gearbox oil? Many drivers have never done it — some don't even know it needs changing.

Gearbox oil (also known as ATF — Automatic Transmission Fluid) is the lifeblood of your car's automatic transmission. Without regular replacement, your gearbox will fail — and repairs can run into thousands of ringgit.

## When Should You Change Your Gearbox Oil?

| Driving Conditions | Change Interval |
|---|---|
| Normal driving (highway) | Every 60,000–80,000 km |
| City driving (stop-start) | Every 40,000–60,000 km |
| Never changed before | Change now |
| Bought a used car | Change now |

### Signs Your Gearbox Oil Needs Changing

- Oil colour is dark or black (it should be clear red)
- Burnt smell
- Delayed gear engagement
- Vibration or jerking when shifting gears
- RPM climbs but the car accelerates slowly

If you experience any of these symptoms, **don't wait** — every day you drive with degraded oil adds more damage to internal components.

## ATF Oil vs CVT Oil — Don't Mix Them Up!

This is a common mistake:

- **ATF oil** — For conventional automatic transmissions (with torque converter)
- **CVT oil** — For CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)

Using ATF oil in a CVT car can **destroy the belt and pulley within weeks**. Always confirm your car's transmission type.
